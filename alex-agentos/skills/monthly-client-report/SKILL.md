# Monthly Client Report

Owners don't buy dashboards — they buy understanding and momentum. Translate numbers into meaning and a decision. Match the client's `reporting.md` tone (most local owners want plain language, not jargon).

## Inputs
- The period's metrics (spend, leads, cost-per-lead, bookings, cost-per-booked, revenue attributed, content/engagement, organic).
- Last period's numbers for deltas. What the agency actually did. The client's KPIs from `CLAUDE.md`.

## Structure (write in this order, plain language)
1. **What happened** — the headline numbers for the period, vs last period and vs target.
2. **What changed** — the meaningful deltas (up/down), not every metric.
3. **What it means** — interpretation: why it moved, what's working, what isn't. This is the value — never skip it.
4. **What we did** — the work delivered this period, tied to results.
5. **What we recommend next** — the 1-3 moves that matter, each with the expected impact.
6. **Next period's priorities** — the concrete plan for the coming weeks.
7. **Simple summary** — 3-4 sentences a busy owner reads in 30 seconds and feels in control.

## Rules
- Lead with cost-per-booked / revenue impact, not vanity metrics (impressions, reach) unless they explain a result.
- Be honest about what underperformed and what you're changing — trust compounds.
- One decision per recommendation. No 12-item lists.
- Numbers get context ("CPL down 22% to €14, below the €18 target") — never bare figures.

## Output
The 7 sections above, formatted from `templates/monthly-report-template.md`, ready to send.

## Never
- Dump metrics without interpretation.
- Hide bad results or bury them in jargon.
- Recommend everything — recommend the few things that move revenue.
