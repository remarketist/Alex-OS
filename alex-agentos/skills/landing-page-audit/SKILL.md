# Landing Page Audit

A scored teardown. If given a URL, fetch it and evaluate the real page. Judge it as a cold visitor who just clicked an ad — not as a designer.

## Score each dimension 1-5 (5 = converts)
1. **Above the fold** — in 5s: what is this, is it for me, what do I get, what do I do? Message-matches the ad?
2. **Offer clarity** — is the offer specific, valuable, and instantly understood?
3. **CTA strength** — one primary action, obvious, repeated, low-friction verb?
4. **Proof / trust** — reviews, before/afters (compliant), credentials, real photos, guarantees, location proof?
5. **Visual hierarchy** — does the eye land on offer → proof → CTA in order? Or is it noise?
6. **Mobile experience** — tap targets, form length, load speed, sticky CTA, no pinch-zoom?
7. **Conversion path** — how many steps/fields to convert? Every one is a leak.
8. **Ad-message alignment** — does the page keep the exact promise the ad made?
9. **Tracking** — is the primary conversion event actually tracked (form/call/booking)?

## Scoring
- Total /45. Under 27 = leaking money badly; 27-36 = fixable wins; 37+ = optimize edges.
- The lowest 2-3 dimensions ARE the priority list.

## Output
- **Score table** — each dimension /5 with the one-line reason
- **Conversion diagnosis** — the single biggest killer (usually offer clarity or above-the-fold match, not aesthetics)
- **Priority fixes** — ranked, each with the business reason and rough conversion impact
- **Revised above-the-fold** — headline + subhead + CTA rewritten
- **Revised page structure** — section-by-section blueprint a builder can implement
- **Tracking gaps** — what must be instrumented before spending on traffic

## Never
- Score on looks. A pretty page that doesn't answer the 5-second questions scores low.
- Recommend a fix without the revenue reason.
