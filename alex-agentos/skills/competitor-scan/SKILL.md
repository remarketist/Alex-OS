# Competitor Scan

Goal: not a competitor summary — the unclaimed angle the client can own. Every finding ends in "…therefore".

## Gather (use WebSearch / WebFetch; label anything unverified)
- Real competitive set: same catchment + same buying cycle (not just same category). 4-7 competitors.
- For each: website + landing structure, Google Business Profile, review text, Meta Ad Library angles (search "<brand> Meta Ad Library" or facebook.com/ads/library), pricing/booking flow, social content.

## Extract
1. **Positioning** — the promise each makes; cluster them to find the crowded messages.
2. **Offers & guarantees** — packages, first-visit deals, risk-reversal, financing.
3. **CTAs & funnels** — how they capture (form/WhatsApp/call/booking), speed, follow-up.
4. **Pricing signals** — transparent vs hidden, anchors, "from $X".
5. **Proof** — reviews (mine the exact words customers use — that becomes ad copy), before/afters, credentials, volume claims.
6. **Content & ad angles** — recurring hooks and emotional drivers; note what NONE of them use.

## Synthesize
- **What everyone says** — crowded messages to avoid.
- **What nobody says** — valuable, unclaimed messages (pulled from real customer language and unmet complaints).
- **The gap** — the open territory.

## Output
- **Competitor map** — the set, one-line positioning each
- **Offer comparison table** — offer / guarantee / price posture / CTA / proof
- **What everyone says / what nobody says**
- **Positioning gaps** — the open territory
- **Strategic opening** — the space to own
- **Recommended angle** — the specific message + why it wins here, ready to hand to offer/ads/content agents

## Never
- List competitors without the strategic implication.
- Mistake visual style for positioning.
- Recommend copying — find the gap.
